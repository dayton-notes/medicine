### Prenatal

#### general

Visit Schedule (Avg. risk)

- Initial visit at 6-8 weeks
- Every 4 weeks until 28 weeks
- Every 2 weeks until 36 weeks
- Weekly until delivery

Newer ACOG guidance generally allows for less frequent visits / telehealth

#### medications in pregnancy

🚫 **Medications Commonly Contraindicated in Pregnancy**

1. **Retinoids**

- **Isotretinoin** (Accutane, Absorica, Claravis): High risk of birth defects.

- **Topical retinoids** (e.g., tretinoin, adapalene): Best avoided.

2. **Statins**

- **Atorvastatin, Simvastatin, Lovastatin**: Previously Category X; may still pose risks like miscarriage.

3. **NSAIDs (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs)**

- **Ibuprofen, Naproxen, Diclofenac**: Risk of kidney and heart issues in fetus, especially after 20 weeks.

4. **Antibiotics**

- **Tetracyclines** (e.g., doxycycline): Can cause tooth discoloration and bone growth issues.

- **Fluoroquinolones** (e.g., ciprofloxacin): Potential risks; limited human data.

- **Sulfonamides** (e.g., sulfamethoxazole/trimethoprim): Risk of embryo-fetal toxicity.

5. **Antiseizure Medications**

- **Valproic acid, Carbamazepine, Topiramate**: Associated with birth defects like spina bifida and cleft palate.

6. **Blood Pressure Medications**

- **ACE inhibitors** (e.g., lisinopril) and **ARBs** (e.g., losartan): Can harm fetal kidneys and amniotic fluid levels.

7. **Anticoagulants**

- **Warfarin**: Risk of miscarriage, stillbirth, and fetal abnormalities.

8. **Psychotropic Medications**

- **Lithium**: Risk of cardiac defects.

- **Benzodiazepines** (e.g., alprazolam, clonazepam): Linked to developmental issues and withdrawal in newborns.

9. **Hormonal Agents**

- **Oral contraceptives, hormone replacement therapies**: Contraindicated.

- **Mifepristone**: Used for medical abortion.

10. **Dermatologic Agents**

- **Finasteride, Acitretin, Tazarotene**: Risk of fetal abnormalities.

11. **Decongestants**

- **Pseudoephedrine (Sudafed)**: Avoid in first trimester due to potential birth defects.

12. **Cancer Chemotherapies**

- **Methotrexate, Thalidomide, Lenalidomide**: Highly teratogenic.

#### recommended weight gain

---

**Weight Gain Guidelines for Singleton Pregnancies**

| **BMI Category**         | **BMI Range**       | **Recommended Weight Gain** |

|--------------------------|---------------------|------------------------------|

| **Underweight**          | < 18.5              | 28–40 lbs (13–18 kg)         |

| **Normal weight**        | 18.5–24.9           | 25–35 lbs (11–16 kg)         |

| **Overweight**           | 25.0–29.9           | 15–25 lbs (7–11 kg)          |

| **Obese**                | ≥ 30.0              | 11–20 lbs (5–9 kg)           |

---

**Weight Gain Guidelines for Twin Pregnancies**

| **BMI Category**         | **BMI Range**       | **Recommended Weight Gain** |

|--------------------------|---------------------|------------------------------|

| **Underweight**          | < 18.5              | 50–62 lbs (22.7–28.1 kg)\*   |

| **Normal weight**        | 18.5–24.9           | 37–54 lbs (16.8–24.5 kg)     |

| **Overweight**           | 25.0–29.9           | 31–50 lbs (14.1–22.7 kg)     |

| **Obese**                | ≥ 30.0              | 25–42 lbs (11.3–19.1 kg)     |

\*Note: The recommendation for underweight women with twins is less well-established and may vary.

---

**Trimester-Based Weight Gain (for Normal BMI)**

- **First trimester**: ~1–4.5 lbs total

- **Second & third trimesters**: ~1 lb/week

#### labs and imaging

- Initial prenatal (CBC, CMP, Type and screen, RH, STI (GC/CT, RPR, HIV, Hep B/C), Titers (Rubella / Varicella), UA w/ culture, Pap (if due), TSH/Glucose/A1C (if risk factors), hemoglobin electrophoresis

- Viability ultrasound: IUP and establish EDD

- 1st Trimester: Serum hcg, PAPP-A, cell free DNA (after 10 weeks)

- 18-22 Weeks: Quad Screen (AFP, HCG, estriol, inhibin A) or Mat21
	- Anatomy Ultrasound

- 26-28 Weeks:  RPR, HIV, OGTT, CBC, Rhogam (if -), TDAP
	- Tubal ligation consent (medicaid)
- 36 Weeks: GC/CT, GBS, Repeat HIV/HepB/C, CBC

3rd Trimester (if obese / gestational diabetes)

- Fetal growth ultrasounds every 3-4 weeks

### [[pre-eclampsia]]

Hypertension (>140/>90) + another feature

- Most Common: proteinuria (>1+ qual or >0.3 Protein/Cr ratio)
- New onset headache not relieved by meds
- Vision changes (scotomata / photopsia)
- Shortness of breath (2/2 pulmonary edema, apart from normal)
- Thrombocytopenia (<100)
- RUQ pain (2xULN ALT/AST)

Also check for reflexes, clonus, edema

Severe Features

- BP >160 / >110
- Thrombocytopenia
- HELLP
- Seizure (eclampsia!)

#### workup

- CBC
- CMP or BMP + LFT
- LDH
- UA
- Spot urine Protein / Cr

#### Treatment

- IV Mg (4g bolus then 1g per hour for 24hrs)
- Lower BP <160/<110
	- BP Meds safe in pregnancy: Nifedipine, Hydralazine, Labetolol
