[[Penicillins]]
	[[Natural Penicillins]] (strep, syphilis)
	[[Ampicillin]], [[Amoxicillin]] (strep, Enterococcus, Listeria)
	[[Nafcillin]], oxacillin (staph)
	Unasyn, Augmentin (mixed – no Pseudomonas)
	[[Zosyn]] (mixed – including Pseudomonas)

Cephalosporins
	Cefazolin, cephalexin (staph, strep, PECK)
	Ceftriaxone (strep, gram-negatives, Not Pseudomonas)
	Ceftazidime (gram-neg, including Pseudomonas)
	Cefepime (gram-neg, including Pseudomonas)
	Oral 2nd & 3rd generation (resp tract; some UTIs)

Monobactams
	[[Aztreonam]] (gram-neg, PCN allergy, expensive)

 Carbapenems
	Imipenem & [[Meropenem]] (mixed, broad)
	Ertapenem (mixed, but not as broad, no pseudomonas)

Beta-Lactams that hit [[Pseudomonas]]
	Piperacillin/tazobactam
	Ceftazidime
	Cefepime
	Aztreonam
	Meropenem
	Imipenem
	Ceftolozane/tazobactam (Zerbaxa)
	Ceftazidime/avibactam (Avycaz)
	Meropenem/Vaborbactam

Adverse Effects
	Hypersensitivity:  rash & anaphylaxis
	GI: diarrhea, nausea & vomiting; C. difficile infection
	Seizures (especially carbapenems in high doses &/or risk factors)
		This is probably the most common “dose-related” adverse effect of beta-lactams
