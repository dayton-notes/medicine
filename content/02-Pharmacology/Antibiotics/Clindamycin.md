Pathogen coverage:
	S. aureus (2nd line); used often for community acquired-MRSA (D-test negative)
		D test = inducible resistance
	Strep (2nd line) – 10 - 15% resistance in S. pneumoniae
	Anaerobes (resistance increasing among “belly anaerobes”)
	Toxoplasma, PCP

Clinical Uses:
	Skin, skin structure infections – especially CA-MRSA
	Useful in some bone & joint infections
	Component to target anaerobes (not in the belly) – eg. PID, lung abscess
	Turn off toxin production (eg, necrotizing fasciitis due to group A strep)
	Topical: acne, rosacea, bacterial vaginosis

Pearls:
	Think G+ and Anaerobes
	Down-regulates toxin production
	Available IV/PO/IM/Topical
	C. diff risk
