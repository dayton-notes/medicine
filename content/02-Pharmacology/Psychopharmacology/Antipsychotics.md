### Options



### Positive & Negative Symptom Management 

Positive Symptoms: 
- Hallucinations, delusions, thought disorder.
- Often managed by First-Generation Antipsychotics (FGAs) which block dopamine D2 receptors.

Negative Symptoms: 
- Flat affect, anhedonia, avolition, alogia. 
- Second-Generation Antipsychotics (SGAs) (blocking both D2 and 5-HT2A) may have better efficacy for these symptoms.
### Precautions and Counseling Points 
**FGAs**
- Monitor closely for EPS (dystonia, akathisia, pseudoparkinsonism, tardive dyskinesia)
- NMS
- Hyperprolactinemia
- Use caution in patients with cardiac arrhythmias (QT prolongation). 
- Counsel to report muscle stiffness or tremors early. 
**SGAs**
- Higher risk of metabolic side effects (weight gain, dyslipidemia, hyperglycemia, T2DM). 
- Emphasize diet, exercise, and routine metabolic monitoring (BMI, lipids, glucose).
- Some risk of EPS/hyperprolactinemia (e.g., risperidone)
