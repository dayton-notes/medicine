### Opioid

#### quick reference

##### Adult Doses:
Morphine-
- (IV): 2–4 mg IV every 2–4 hours as needed
- (oral): 10–15 mg PO every 4 hours as needed

Fentanyl-
- (IV): 50-100 mcg IV every 30–60 minutes as needed.

Hydromorphone 
- (IV): 0.2–1 mg IV every 2–3 hours as needed
- (oral): 2–4 mg PO every 4–6 hours as needed

Oxycodone
- Opioid naive: 5-10 mg PO q4hr PRN.
- Opioid tolerant: 10-30 mg PO q4hr PRN

- **Roughly equianalgesic doses**
    - 5 mg IV morphine
    - 15 mg PO morphine (1/3 as strong as IV).
    - 50 mcg IV fentanyl (10x morphine dose in mg).
    - 0.75 mg IV HYDROmorphone (7x more potent than IV morphine).
    - 6 mg IV nalbuphine.
    - 3.75 mg PO HYDROmorphone (4x more potent than PO morphine).
    - 10 mg oxycodone (1.5x stronger than PO morphine).
    - 15 mg HYDROcodone (1.5x weaker than PO oxycodone).
    - 150 mg tramadol (10x less potent than PO morphine).

STOP-ACT:
Acute pain: 5 days 
Post op: 7 days
General: <90 mme / day
- 10mg oxycodone q4 = 90mme
- Non-cancer patients: give w/apap


- When switching between opioids: cut 30-50%
- Duration of IV is shorter than oral (2-3 hours vs 6-12 hours)
- Onset of IV is faster than Oral (minutes vs 30-60 min)
