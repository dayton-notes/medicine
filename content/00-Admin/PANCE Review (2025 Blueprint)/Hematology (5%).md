# Coagulation Disorders

- **Hemophilia A**

    - Classically presents with **hemarthrosis**
        
    - ↓ Factor VIII
        
- **Hemophilia B**
    
    - “Christmas disease” → Factor IX deficiency
        
    - Clinically indistinguishable from Hemophilia A

# Cytoses

## Polycythemia

- **Primary** (JAK2 mutation)
    
- **Secondary** (e.g., smokers, hypoxia)
    
- No splenomegaly
    

### Polycythemia Vera (PCV)

- JAK2 mutation
    
- Aquagenic pruritus
    
- Erythromelalgia (red hands)
    
- Ruddy cyanosis
    
- ↑ LDH
    
- **Treatment:** phlebotomy, low-dose aspirin
    

## Thrombocytosis

- Usually reactive
    
- Essential thrombocythemia → JAK2 mutation
    
- ↑ risk of PE/DVT
    
- Risk of AML transformation

# Hereditary Disorders

## Hereditary Spherocytosis

- Most common in Northern Europeans
    
- Pigmented gallstones
    
- Hyperchromic anemia, ↑ RDW, ↑ MCHC
    
- **Diagnosis:** osmotic fragility test
    

## G6PD Deficiency

- Most common in males (African, Mediterranean)
    
- ↑ sensitivity to oxidative stress:
    
    - Fava beans
        
    - Infections
        
    - Medications (sulfa, nitrofurantoin, phenazopyridine)
        

## Hemochromatosis

- Total body iron overload
    
- Bronzed skin, arthralgias
    
- Genetic testing (HFE gene)
    

---

# Sickle Cell Disease

- Functional asplenia → ↑ risk of encapsulated organisms
    
- **Prophylaxis:** antibiotics
    

## Complications

- **Osteomyelitis:** Salmonella
    
- **Aplastic crisis:** Parvovirus B19
    
- **Microthrombosis**
    
    - Avascular necrosis (long bone heads)
        
    - H-shaped vertebrae
        
    - Priapism
        

## Management

- Hydroxyurea
    
- Folate supplementation
    

## Acute Chest Syndrome

- New lung infiltrate + fever, cough, chest pain, tachypnea
    
- Leading cause of death
    
- **Treatment:**
    
    - Oxygen
        
    - IV fluids
        
    - Analgesia
        
    - ± Exchange transfusion

# Thrombotic Microangiopathies

## Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (TTP)

- **Pentad:**
    
    - MAHA
        
    - Thrombocytopenia
        
    - Neurologic symptoms
        
    - Renal dysfunction
        
    - Fever
        

## Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS)

- MAHA
    
- Thrombocytopenia
    
- Renal insufficiency
    
- Classically in children after diarrheal illness
