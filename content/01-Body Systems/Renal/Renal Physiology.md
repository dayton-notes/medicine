### Intrinsic Regulation - GFR Maintenance

**Purpose: Maintain GFR- H2O and Solute Homeostasis**
- Mechanism: Smooth muscle contraction in Afferent Arteriole / Macula Densa

State: GFR too high
Solution: Constrict afferent arteriole
- Triggered by:
1. Afferent arteriole stretch (mildly elevated BP): too much blood going into glomerulus
2. High [NaCl] concentration : too much tubular fluid solute
- Reaction:
1. Myogenic Response: Intra-arteriole Ca2+ release causes SMC contraction in afferent arteriole
2. Tubuloglomerular Mechanism: Macula Densa releases “vasoactive magic stuff” that constricts afferent arteriole

State- GFR too low
Solution- Relax afferent arteriole / Constrict efferent arteriole
- Triggered by:
1. Afferent arteriole shrinkage (mildly lowered BP): not enough blood going into glomerulus
2. Low [NaCl] concentration : not enough tubular fluid solute
- Reaction:
1. Myogenic Response: SMC relaxes in afferent arteriole
2. Tubuloglomerular Mechanism: Macula Densa releases prostaglandin/renin, that constricts efferent arteriole

### Extrinsic Regulation - BP Maintenance
**Purpose: Maintain BP- something bad happened (BP/Blood Volume (BV) too low!)**
Mechanisms: Hormones and Sympathetic Nervous System

State- BP/blood volume too high
Solution- Constrict afferent arteriole
- Triggered by:
1. R Atrium Stretch: ANP released
2. High [NaCl] concentration: too much tubular fluid solute
- Reaction:
1. Myogenic Response: Intra-arteriole Ca2+ release causes SMC contraction in afferent arteriole
2. Tubuloglomerular Mechanism: Macula Densa releases “vasoactive magic stuff” that constricts afferent arteriole

State- BP/blood volume too low
Solution- Relax afferent arteriole / Constrict efferent arteriole
- Sympathetic (neurotransmitter or circulating catecholamine), e.g., hemorrhaging
- Granular cells release renin.
- Constrict afferent (far more a1 receptors) more than efferent arterioles (fewer a1 receptors)to decrease filtration to conserve blood volume.
- This also indirectly triggers the Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone system.
