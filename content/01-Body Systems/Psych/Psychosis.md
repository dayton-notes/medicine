### Core Clinical Features of Psychosis

| Symptom                         | Description                                                                                             | Notes                                                               |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Delusions                       | Fixed beliefs not amenable to change in light of conflicting evidence                                   | Persecutory, grandiose, somatic, etc.                               |
| Hallucinations                  | Perception-like experiences without external stimuli (auditory, visual, tactile, gustatory, olfactory). | Often auditory in schizophrenia.                                    |
| Disorganized Speech             | Incoherent “word salad,” tangential or circumstantial speech, thought blocking.                         | Jumping between topics or abruptly stopping mid thought.            |
| Disorganized/Catatonic Behavior | Unpredictable agitation, lack of response, rigid posturing, purposeless movements.                      | May involve mutism, stupor, or purposeless motor activity.          |
| Negative Symptoms               | Diminished emotional expression, avolition, anhedonia, alogia, asociality.                              | Often appear as flat affect, reduced motivation, social withdrawal. |

### Types and Duration  

| Disorder                 | Duration                         |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| Delusional Disorder      | Delusions ≥ 1 month              |
| Brief Psychotic Disorder | 1 day– 1 month                   |
| Schizophreniform         | 1– 6 months                      |
| Schizophrenia            | ≥ 6 months                       |
| Schizoaffective Disorder | Chronic; also has mood component |

### Clinical Presentation of Specific Disorders

**Delusional Disorder:** 
- One or more delusions for at least one month (e.g. persecutory, jealous, grandiose). 
**Brief Psychotic Disorder:** 
- Delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, or catatonic behavior for at least 1 day but less than 1 month. 
**Schizophreniform Disorder:** 
- Same core symptoms as schizophrenia (delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, disorganized/catatonic behavior, negative symptoms), lasting 1–6 months. 
**Schizophrenia:** 
- Same core symptoms, lasting at least 6 months, with 1 month of active-phase symptoms. 
**Schizoaffective Disorder:** 
- Major mood episode + psychotic symptoms; at least 2 weeks of psychotic symptoms in the absence of a major mood episode
