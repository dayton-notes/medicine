### Angiotensin 2
- Angiotensin II constricts both arterioles. But efferent arterioles have higher sensitivity to Angiotensin II.
- At low dosage, it constricts efferent arterioles, thus raises glomerular hydrostatic pressure while reducing renal blood flow. 
- At high dosage, it constricts both.
### Aldosterone
- Made in the adrenal cortex
- Targets DCT
- Increases reabsorption of sodium
- Decreases plasma potassium, and hydrogen
- Released when low Na+, low blood volume, high K+ or acidosis
