### Overview

**Seizure Definition:**
- transient occurrence of signs &/or symptoms [semiology] due to abnormal excessive or synchronous neuronal activity in the brain
- Sterotypic for patient!

**Seizure Imitators:**
- Psychogenic Non-epileptic spells/seizures (PNES)/Functional Neurologic Disorder (FND)/Functional Seizures (FS)
- Convulsive syncope
	- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOsNeUg1iGA
- Migraine variants
- Some sleep disorders (cataplexy/narcolepsy)
- Movement disorders
- TIAs vs Todd’s Paralysis
- Cardiac arrhythmias
- Vertigo

**Seizure Types**
- Tonic = tight/stiffening
- Clonic = rhythmic shaking/jerking (usually high frequency, low to moderate amplitude)
- Myoclonic = brief jerking spasm of a muscle or group of muscles
- Nonmotor = staring/behavior arrest or sensory only with/without retained awareness
- Motor = has some movement associated with the semiology
- Bilateral tonic-clonic = both sides of body have stiffening then progresses to rapid rhythmic shaking/jerking
- Focal to bilateral tonic-clonic = starts with a focal seizure (motor on only 1 side or nonmotor coming from one side of cortex) and spread to encompass all of cortex with bilateral stiffening -> jerking
