## Pearls

[[#Reading EKGs]]

- need for rate treatment is determined by VENTRICULAR rate
- best lead for P-waves: V1
- A-fib w/ WPW: ONLY shock or procainamide (rate control will KILL)
- AIVR = common reperfusion rhythm s/p PCI/lytics in ACS
	- NOT dangerous - can last for a few minutes
	- Don't treat
- V-fib: CHECK FOR PULSE
- Short PR DDX: junctional rhythm, pre-excitation

#### rhythms

| **Category**                            | **Potential Rhythms**                                                                                                 |
| --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bradycardia**                         | Sinus Brady,  AV Blocks                                                                                               |
| **Narrow Regular Tachycardia**          | Sinus Tach / SVT / A-Flutter                                                                                          |
| **Narrow Irregular Tachycardia**        | A-Fib / A-Flutter w/ variable conduction (F-waves) / MAT (think COPD)                                                 |
| **Wide Regular Tachycardia**            | Treat all as V-Tach                                                                                                   |
| **Wide Irregular Tachycardia**          | A-Fib w/ BBB; A-Fib w/ pre-excitation (e.g. WPW) — rates >200, irregularly wide/narrow, tx: Procainamide OR shock     |
| **Monomorphic Ventricular Tachycardia** | Stable? → Amiodarone; Unstable? → Synchronized cardioversion. AIVR = ventricular rhythm 40–130 bpm (seen post-arrest) |
| **Polymorphic Ventricular Tachycardia** | Long QT → Torsades de Pointes → IV Mg!!                                                                               |

#### DDx for Various Findings

**Anterior T-wave Inversion**
1. abnormal depolarization (make sure its not the QRS' fault - abnormal depolarization = abnormal repolariztion)
2. Anterior reperfusion (i.e. Wellen's syndrome)
3. Acute Right Heart Strain ([[Pulmonary Embolism|PE]], etc)
4. Hypokalemia
5. Takotsubo Cardiomyopathy

## Reading EKGs
### EKG Tracing Overview 
![[Pasted image 20250603103241.png]]
	Interval Time Standards:
	- 1 small box = 40ms (.04 sec)
	- 1 big box = 200ms (.2 sec)
	- PRS- 3-5 small boxes (<1 big box)
	- QRS- <3 small boxes (100-120ms)
	- QT- <440ms (460ms in female)

### Step by Step EKG Interpretation:

1.      Calculate Rate
	- 300 - 150 - 100 - 75 - 60 - 50
2.     Assess Rhythm
	- Is it regular or irregular?
	- If irregular –
		- Is there a pattern or is it irregularly irregular?
		- Are there premature or escape beats?  Based on morphology - what type?
	- Are P waves present?
		- Are they upright/normal in Lead II?
		- Are they similar?
		- Check P:QRS ratio – if not 1:1, check for AV blocks
	- Calculate intervals
		- PR interval – 0.12 to 0.20 sec (3 to 5 small, or < 1 big box)
		- QRS duration 
			- Narrow or wide? (<0.10 or >0.12 sec {3 small boxes})
			- If consistently wide, is there a ventricular arrhythmia? Bundle branch block?
				- (MARROW) RBBB – RSR’ in V1-V2 with wide slurred S in V5-V6
				- (WILLIAM) LBBB – Dominant S in V1 with broad R waves in V5-V6
		- QT interval
			- Normal - ≤ ½ R-R interval (doesn’t work in extremes of HR)
	- Use rhythm information plus rate to name rhythm (i.e. what is setting HR) **_and if present_** any conduction abnormalities
3.     Determine axis
	- Use Leads I and aVF to identify quadrant
		- Normal – positive in Leads I and aVF
		- LAD – positive Lead I, negative Lead aVF
		- RAD – negative Lead I, positive Lead aVF
		- Extreme RAD – negative in Leads I and aVF
		- Axis lies 90 degrees to most biphasic limb lead in identified quadrant
			- Lead I = 0
			- Lead II =   +60
			- Lead III =  +120
			- Lead aVL =  -30
			- Lead aVR =  -150
			- Lead aVF =  +90
4.     Check for hypertrophy
	- Atrial – look for biphasic, diphasic, peaked P waves in Leads II and V1
		- LAE
			- diphasic P wave in Leads II or III, > 120 msec (3 small boxes or more)
			- biphasic P wave in V1 with negative portion last that is  >40 msec long and >1mm deep (1 small box by 1 small box)
		- RAE
			- P wave > 2.5 mm tall in Leads II, III, or aVF  (2.5 small boxes)
			- P wave > 1.5 mm tall in V1  (1.5 small boxes)
	- Ventricular
		- LVH
			- Narrow QRS
			- S wave in V1 + R wave in V5 or V6 (whichever larger) sums to >35 mm (7 big boxes)
		- RVH
			- Narrow QRS
			- RAD > 110
			- R wave in V1 > 7 mm (7 small boxes) or R/S ratio>1
			- S wave in V5 or V6 >7 mm or R/S ratio <1
5.     Look for Ischemia/Injury/Infarction
	- Need to see changes in at least 2 contiguous leads which are:
		- II, III, avF
		- I, aVL, V5, V6
		- V1-V2 and V3-V4
	- Look for T wave inversions, ST depression, ST elevation, pathologic Q waves in all leads
	- STEMI Localization Table


| **LEADS**                                                | **WALL (LV)**                                                                  | **ARTERY**    |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------- |
| II, III, aVF                                             | Inferior                                                                       | RCA > LCX     |
| I, aVL, V5, V6                                           | Lateral                                                                        | LCX           |
| I, aVL                                                   | High Lateral                                                                   | LCX branch    |
| V1-V2                                                    | Septal                                                                         | LAD or branch |
| V3-V4                                                    | Anterior                                                                       | LAD           |
| V1-V3                                                    | Posterior<br><br>(in mirror image – R waves / early transition, ST depression) | RCA           |
| V4R – V6R<br><br>(precordial leads placed on right side) | Right ventricle<br><br>(usually with II, III, aVF)                             | RCA           |

RCA = right coronary artery;  LCX = left circumflex artery;  LAD = left anterior descending artery
