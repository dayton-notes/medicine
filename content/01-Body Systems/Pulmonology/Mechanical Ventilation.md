### Pediatric
[Intro to Pediatric Mechanical Ventilation](https://mededucation.stanford.edu/courses/introduction-to-pediatric-mechanical-ventilation/lessons/introduction-13/)

**3 Major Indications**
- failure to oxygenate (hypoxemic respiratory failrure)
	- ARDS, Pneumonia (V/Q mismatch)
- failure to ventilate (hypercarbic respiratory failure)
	- neuro, asthma, etc
- inability to protect airway
	- GCS<8, AMS, lack of cough/gag reflex

**Ventilation**
- measured by ETCO2 / blood ga
- Determined by RR and Tidal Volume
	- increased RR = decreased Co2  //  big breath = more CO2 released
	- 5-8cc/kg = normal tidal volume

**Oxygenation**
- FiO2 (fraction of inspired O2)
	- .21-1.00 (.21 = room air)
	- FiO2 > .5-.6 can lead to oxygen toxicity if used for long periods
- Mean Airway Pressure (Paw)
	- influences alveolar recruitment (more pressure = more recruitment = more durface area)
	- influence by iTime, eTime, and PIP
		- iTime:eTime = 1:2 normally
		- PIP = peak inspiratory pressure
			- Normal PIP = <25
			- PIP > 30 = risk of barotrauma
		- PEEP
			- minimizes alveolar collapse (closing pressure)
			- reflects FRC
			- Normal = 5cmH2O

**Modes**
Assist Control
SIMV: initiates mechanical breaths and support patient breaths
Pressure Control
- Set pressure, volume depends on lung compliance
Volume Control
- Set volume, pressure depends on lung compliance
