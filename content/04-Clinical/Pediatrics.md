#### Red Flags
- Vital Signs
- Dehydration
	- <3 wet diapers in 24hrs, sticky mucous membranes, slow cap refill
- Respiratory Distress
	- tachypnea, retractions, nostril flaring
- AMS (lethargic!)
- Fever >5 days

#### Newborns and Infants
- Neonatal Hx (<2 weeks of age)
	- Name
	- Pregnancy complications
		- Mental Health / Gestational Diabetes / Lupus
		- ID: Group BStrep / G/C / HIV / Syphilis / Herpes / Hepatitis
	- Fam Hx
	- Prior pregnancies and outcomes
- PE
	- Listen to Heart/Lungs
	- Skull fontanelles / ears
	- Palpate clavicles
	- Bilat grip reflex
	- Listen to Belly
	- Press on belly
	- Undo diaper: femoral pulses, check genitalia, check hips
	- Reattach diaper, check feet reflexes and cap refill
	- Flip baby, check spine
	- Moro reflex
	- Swaddle
	- Mouth: light, gloved finger checks palate and suck reflex
	- RED REFLEX
	- Note skin color during exam
	
- Hyperbilirubinemia
	- Put in notes
	- Phototherapy
	- Goal: prevent bilirubin encephalopathy and Kernicterus
	
- Important PE Findings
	- Scleral hemorrhage (pupil spared = okay)
	- Choanal atresia (lack of patent nasal passages)
	- Torticollis (asymmetrical neck muscle tightness)
	- Murmurs (PDA, ASD/VSD, etc.)
	- Hips (@4-6 weeks: bartlow-adduction / ortolani-abduction) 
		- Click / clunk or breech birth = ultrasound
		- fix= Pavlic harness
	- Feet - club feet
	- Sacral dimples / hair tufts
	- Neuro: symmetric reflexes, normal tone, etc
		- Babinski (present in first 1-2 yrs)
		- Moro (present in <6mo)
		- Grip
		- Suckle (rooting)
	- skin: 
		- milia, dermal melanosis (sacral), nevus simples (stork bite), pustular melanosis
- Red Flags:
	-Neonate
	- hypoglycemia 
	- hyperbilirubinemia
	- weight loss (>10%)
		- normal for some weight loss in first few days
		- lactation consultant
	- jittery and inconsolable
		- withdrawal? drug screen, neonatal abstinence score
	- congenital abnormalities
	-Infants
	- fever > 4days
	- refusing eat/drink, decreased urine output
	- hard to wake / AMS / lethargic / inconsolable by caregiver
- Infant Illnesses
	- Colic - 10wks to 4mo
		- paroxysmal abdominal pain
	- Pyloric Stenosis
		- projectile vomit / bilious emesis
		- olive like mass in mid-epigastrium (probably won't feel it!!)
		- seen on US
		- fix = surgery! (not an emergency)
	- Duodenal Atresia 
		- first 48hrs - congenital duodenal malformation
		- bilious vomiting
		- "double bubble sign"
		- fix = surgery (emergent)
	- Volvulus
		- <6mo
		- recurrent bile stained vomit
